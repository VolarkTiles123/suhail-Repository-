# TILES KNOWLEDGE BASE

## Product Categories

### Porcelain Tiles
- **Definition:** High-fired ceramic with <0.5% water absorption
- **Durability:** Highest (10-15 year lifespan+)
- **Applications:** All (indoor, outdoor, commercial)
- **Cost:** Premium
- **Characteristics:** Non-porous, stain-resistant, scratch-resistant
- **Best For:** Kitchens, bathrooms, high-traffic areas

### Ceramic Tiles
- **Definition:** Medium-fired clay with 3-10% water absorption
- **Durability:** Moderate (5-10 year lifespan)
- **Applications:** Indoor primarily
- **Cost:** Budget to mid-range
- **Characteristics:** Porous, varied glazes, decorative
- **Best For:** Bathrooms, walls, decorative purposes

### Porcelain Slabs
- **Definition:** Large-format porcelain (120×240cm+)
- **Advantage:** Fewer seams, seamless appearance
- **Cost:** Premium
- **Applications:** Modern, luxury projects
- **Installation:** Requires skilled labor

### Floor Tiles
- **Purpose:** High durability, slip-resistance
- **Thickness:** 12-15mm
- **Finish:** Matte or textured
- **Applications:** Living areas, kitchens, outdoor
- **Maintenance:** Easy to clean

### Wall Tiles
- **Purpose:** Aesthetic, water-resistant
- **Thickness:** 6-10mm (lighter)
- **Finish:** Gloss, matte, decorative
- **Applications:** Bathrooms, kitchens, accent walls
- **Maintenance:** Regular cleaning

### Bathroom Tiles
- **Requirements:** Waterproof, non-slip, aesthetic
- **Types:** Porcelain (recommended), ceramic
- **Common Sizes:** 60×60, 60×120, 80×80
- **Colors:** Whites, grays, earth tones
- **Installation:** Professional recommended

### Kitchen Tiles
- **Requirements:** Stain-resistant, easy to clean, durable
- **Backsplash:** 30×60 or 60×120 (decorative common)
- **Floors:** 120×120 or 120×240 (larger = fewer seams)
- **Thickness:** 9-12mm
- **Finish:** Matte (grip), gloss (modern)

### Outdoor Tiles
- **Requirements:** Weather-resistant, slip-resistant
- **Types:** Porcelain (recommended)
- **Sizes:** Large format (120×240+) preferred
- **Finish:** Textured, anti-slip
- **Installation:** Professional required

## Technical Specifications

### Material Composition
- **Clay:** Primary ingredient (varies by type)
- **Feldspar:** Adds durability
- **Silica:** Binding agent
- **Pigments:** Color creation
- **Glazes:** Surface treatment (ceramic)

### Manufacturing Process
1. **Raw Material Extraction**
2. **Mixing & Grinding**
3. **Pressing/Extrusion**
4. **Drying**
5. **Glazing** (ceramic) or **Polishing** (porcelain)
6. **Firing** (1200°C+ for porcelain, 1000-1100°C for ceramic)
7. **Quality Check**
8. **Packaging**

### Quality Standards
- **ISO 13006** - Ceramic tile specifications
- **PEI Rating** - Hardness/durability (1-5 scale)
- **Slip Resistance** - DCOF ratings
- **Thickness Tolerance** - ±2mm standard
- **Color Variation** - V-rated categories

## Installation Best Practices

### Preparation
- Clean, level, dry substrate required
- Primer for porous surfaces
- Waterproofing for wet areas (bathrooms, kitchens)
- Proper ventilation

### Tools & Materials
- Tile adhesive (cement or epoxy)
- Grout (sanded for wide grout lines)
- Spacers (consistency)
- Sealant (for grout and tiles)

### Process
1. Apply adhesive with notched trowel
2. Place tiles with spacers
3. Allow to set (24 hours)
4. Apply grout
5. Remove grout haze
6. Cure properly (7-14 days)
7. Seal grout lines (optional but recommended)

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ Rushing installation (needs proper curing)
- ❌ Wrong adhesive type
- ❌ Skipping waterproofing in wet areas
- ❌ Inconsistent grout lines
- ❌ Installing without proper substrate prep

## Maintenance & Care

### Regular Cleaning
- Sweep/vacuum regularly
- Damp mop with mild soap
- Avoid acidic cleaners (damage grout)
- Dry after cleaning

### Protection
- Use trivets for hot items
- Coasters for drinks
- Protective pads under furniture
- Rugs in high-traffic areas

### Grout Maintenance
- Seal grout lines (annually recommended)
- Clean grout with pH-neutral cleaner
- Address cracks promptly

### Tile Sealing
- Ceramic: Usually not needed (glazed)
- Porcelain: Optional (unsealed is fine)
- Natural stone: Recommended

### Lifespan
- **Well-maintained:** 10-15+ years
- **Standard use:** 5-10 years
- **High-traffic:** 5-8 years (with proper maintenance)
